TOP SHOWS

Modern family

"Modern Family" is a popular American sitcom that offers a humorous and heartfelt look into the lives of the Pritchett-Dunphy-Tucker clan, a large and diverse extended family. The show is known for its unique mockumentary style, where characters frequently break the fourth wall to speak directly to the camera. The series features: Jay Pritchett, the patriarch, who is married to the much younger Gloria, a vivacious Colombian woman, and stepfather to her son Manny. Claire Dunphy, Jay's daughter, who is a stay-at-home mom married to Phil, a well-meaning but often clueless realtor. They have three children: the rebellious Haley, the smart but socially awkward Alex, and the quirky Luke. Mitchell Pritchett, Jay's son, who is a lawyer married to Cameron Tucker. They have an adopted daughter, Lily, and their relationship brings a lot of warmth and humor to the show. "Modern Family" has been praised for its witty writing, strong ensemble cast, and its portrayal of various family dynamics and modern-day relationships. The show successfully combines humor with heartfelt moments, making it relatable and enjoyable for a wide audience. Throughout its eleven-season run, "Modern Family" has received numerous awards and accolades, cementing its place as one of the most beloved sitcoms of the 21st century.

inside out 2

"Inside Out 2" is the eagerly awaited sequel to Pixar's critically acclaimed animated film "Inside Out." The original movie, released in 2015, delved into the inner workings of a young girl named Riley's mind, personifying her emotions—Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust—as characters navigating the complexities of her experiences. In "Inside Out 2," the story picks up as Riley enters her teenage years, a period filled with new emotions and challenges. The sequel promises to introduce a fresh array of emotions to the ensemble, reflecting the growing complexity of Riley's inner world as she faces the ups and downs of adolescence. The core emotions from the first film—Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust—return, continuing their roles in guiding Riley through this transformative stage of life.

How to make millions before grandma dies

Don’t let its wry title fool you—truth be told, How to Make Millions Before Grandma Dies (2024) is a deeply sentimental movie that’s sure to pull at your heartstrings. Pat Boonnitipat’s latest film, which has already become one of the highest-grossing domestic Thai flicks, is a charming tale of family, kinship and second chances. It’s no wonder that this movie has achieved immense success on social media and in cinemas region-wide, given the universal catharsis and charm it offers. In this film, we follow the lives of a multigenerational Thai-Chinese family through the eyes of a teenage grandson named M (played by Thai celebrity Putthipong Assaratanakul, or Billkin). He seeks to curry favour with his terminally ill grandmother, affectionately known as Amah, in hopes of receiving a generous inheritance when she (inevitably) passes. As such, he decides to move in with Amah to be her full-time caregiver.

Dune: Part 2

"Dune: Part 2" is the highly anticipated sequel to Denis Villeneuve's 2021 film adaptation of Frank Herbert's seminal science fiction novel "Dune." Picking up where the first movie left off, this installment delves deeper into the intricate political, ecological, and spiritual conflicts of the desert planet Arrakis. The story continues to follow Paul Atreides, played by Timothée Chalamet, who is on a path to embrace his destiny as the prophesied leader of the Fremen, the desert-dwelling people of Arrakis. With the help of his mother, Lady Jessica (Rebecca Ferguson), Paul seeks to avenge his family's betrayal and reclaim control over the spice-rich planet. The spice, melange, is the most valuable substance in the universe, essential for space travel and extending human life, making Arrakis a focal point of interstellar intrigue and conflict.

K-DRAMAS

True beauty

"True Beauty" is a South Korean television series based on the popular webtoon of the same name. The story follows Lim Ju-kyung, a high school girl who masters the art of makeup to transform her appearance and gain self-confidence. Despite her stunning looks, Ju-kyung's real journey is about discovering her true self and finding beauty within. With charming performances, heartfelt moments, and a dash of humor, "True Beauty" explores themes of self-acceptance, the pressures of societal beauty standards, and the power of inner beauty. The series has resonated with audiences worldwide, making it a must-watch for K-Drama enthusiasts.

Extraordinary attorney Woo

"Extraordinary Attorney Woo" is a South Korean drama that tells the compelling story of Woo Young-woo, a brilliant lawyer with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). Despite facing societal prejudices and the challenges of her condition, Young-woo's extraordinary intellect and keen eye for detail make her an exceptional attorney. The series follows her journey as she navigates complex legal cases, fights for justice, and learns to balance her professional and personal life. With heartfelt performances, insightful storytelling, and a focus on diversity and inclusion, "Extraordinary Attorney Woo" sheds light on the capabilities and potential of individuals with ASD, inspiring viewers with its message of resilience and acceptance.

The atypical family

Bok Gwi-Ju (Jang Ki-Yong) and his family were born with different supernatural powers. Bok Gwi-Ju is able to travel back in time, but only to happy times in his life. He can't change the past, so he can only dwell on those happy moments. Bok Gwi-Ju becomes afflicted with depression and this leads him to lose his supernatural power. His family also loses their own powers due to modern day problems like insomnia, bulimia, and smartphone addiction. One day, Do Da-Hae (Chun Woo-Hee) happens to get involved with Bok Gwi-Ju and his family. She begins to live with them and change occurs.

Sweet home

Sweet Home (Korean: 스위트홈) is a South Korean apocalyptic horror television series starring Song Kang, Lee Jin-wook, and Lee Si-young. It is based on the Naver webtoon of the same name by Kim Carnby and Hwang Young-chan, which recorded over 2.1 billion net views. The first season was released on Netflix on December 18, 2020.[6][7] The second season was released on December 1, 2023.[8] The third season is scheduled to be released on July 19, 2024.[9][10]

COMEDY/ROMCOMS

Young Sheldon

"Young Sheldon" is a successful sitcom prequel to "The Big Bang Theory" that explores the childhood of Sheldon Cooper in East Texas during the late 1980s and early 1990s. Played by Iain Armitage, Sheldon navigates life as a child prodigy, often clashing with his family—his supportive mother Mary (Zoe Perry), football coach father George Sr. (Lance Barber), twin sister Missy (Raegan Revord), and older brother George Jr. (Montana Jordan)—due to his extraordinary intellect and socially awkward demeanor. The show humorously depicts Sheldon's struggles with fitting in socially while highlighting his unique perspective on the world, making it a favorite among fans for its nostalgic setting, heartfelt family dynamics, and witty storytelling rooted in the character's journey from childhood to adolescence.

Two men and a half

"Two and a Half Men" is characterized by its blend of humor ranging from witty banter to situational comedy, often revolving around Charlie's hedonistic lifestyle contrasting with Alan's more conservative nature and Jake's youthful innocence. As the series progresses, the dynamic between the characters evolves through various romantic entanglements, family crises, and comedic misunderstandings. The show underwent a notable change in later seasons when Charlie Sheen's character was written out and replaced by Ashton Kutcher's character, Walden Schmidt, which brought a fresh dynamic to the storyline. Despite controversies surrounding its stars, "Two and a Half Men" remained popular throughout its twelve-season run, becoming one of the longest-running sitcoms on American television and leaving a lasting impact on the genre.

The office

"The Office" is a beloved American television sitcom that originally aired from 2005 to 2013, adapted from the British series created by Ricky Gervais and Stephen Merchant. Set in the Scranton, Pennsylvania branch of the fictional Dunder Mifflin paper company, the show employs a mockumentary format to depict the everyday lives of its quirky and diverse group of office employees. The series stars Steve Carell as Michael Scott, the bumbling and often inappropriate regional manager who strives to be liked by his staff. The ensemble cast includes Rainn Wilson as Dwight Schrute, a dedicated but eccentric salesman; John Krasinski as Jim Halpert, the witty salesman known for his pranks and unrequited love for receptionist Pam Beesly, played by Jenna Fischer. The show is celebrated for its humor, memorable characters, and ability to blend satire with genuine emotional moments, creating a lasting impact on popular culture.

seinfeld

"Seinfeld" is often regarded as a groundbreaking sitcom that revolutionized television comedy with its blend of wit, satire, and relatable characters. Created by Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David, the show primarily revolves around Jerry Seinfeld, a stand-up comedian, and his quirky group of friends in Manhattan. George Costanza, played by Jason Alexander, is Jerry's neurotic best friend whose constant failures and schemes provide comedic fodder. Elaine Benes, portrayed by Julia Louis-Dreyfus, is Jerry's ex-girlfriend turned close friend, known for her sharp wit and assertive personality. Cosmo Kramer, played by Michael Richards, is Jerry's eccentric neighbor whose unpredictable behavior and larger-than-life persona often lead to hilarious situations. "Seinfeld" is famous for its exploration of mundane topics and everyday life scenarios, which are humorously exaggerated and dissected through its ensemble cast's interactions. The show's catchphrases, memorable episodes (like the Soup Nazi and Festivus), and its cultural impact have solidified its legacy as a classic sitcom that continues to influence comedy and television even decades after its original airing.

ROMANCE

The notebook

"The Notebook" is a romantic drama film released in 2004, based on the novel of the same name by Nicholas Sparks. Directed by Nick Cassavetes, the film tells the story of Noah Calhoun and Allie Hamilton, two young lovers who meet one summer in the 1940s in South Carolina. Noah, played by Ryan Gosling, is a poor yet passionate young man, while Allie, portrayed by Rachel McAdams, is a wealthy young woman with societal expectations. Despite their different backgrounds, they fall deeply in love but are torn apart by circumstances and societal pressures. The narrative unfolds through a dual timeline, showing the couple's passionate romance and the challenges they face as they grow older. "The Notebook" is celebrated for its emotional depth, poignant storytelling, and powerful performances, becoming a beloved classic in the romance genre.

The titanic

The Titanic" refers to both the historical event and the 1997 epic romance-disaster film directed by James Cameron. The film portrays the tragic sinking of the RMS Titanic on its maiden voyage in 1912, which claimed the lives of over 1,500 passengers and crew. The storyline focuses on the fictional characters Jack Dawson, played by Leonardo DiCaprio, a penniless artist, and Rose DeWitt Bukater, portrayed by Kate Winslet, a young aristocrat engaged to an arrogant millionaire. Jack and Rose's romance unfolds amidst the luxury and opulence of the Titanic, highlighting class differences and societal expectations. The film's meticulous attention to historical detail, groundbreaking visual effects, and powerful performances made it a critical and commercial success. "Titanic" won numerous awards, including eleven Oscars, and remains one of the highest-grossing films of all time, leaving an indelible mark on popular culture.

Lalaland

"La La Land" is a 2016 musical romantic comedy-drama film written and directed by Damien Chazelle. Set in modern-day Los Angeles, the film stars Emma Stone as Mia Dolan, an aspiring actress, and Ryan Gosling as Sebastian Wilder, a jazz pianist. The story follows their passionate love affair as they pursue their dreams in the bustling cityscape, navigating the challenges of artistic ambition and personal aspirations. "La La Land" pays homage to classic Hollywood musicals while infusing contemporary themes of love, sacrifice, and the pursuit of happiness. The film received widespread acclaim for its enchanting musical numbers, captivating performances, and visually stunning cinematography. It won six Academy Awards, including Best Director for Damien Chazelle, and became a cultural phenomenon for its nostalgic yet modern take on the romance genre.

The summer i turned pretty

"The Summer I Turned Pretty" is a young adult book series written by Jenny Han. It consists of three novels: "The Summer I Turned Pretty" (2009), "It's Not Summer Without You" (2010), and "We'll Always Have Summer" (2011). The series revolves around the protagonist, Isabel "Belly" Conklin, and her coming-of-age experiences during summers spent at a beach house with her mother and brother, and their close family friends, the Fishers. Throughout the series, Belly reflects on her childhood crush on Conrad Fisher, one of the Fisher boys, and her evolving relationships with him and his brother Jeremiah. Each summer brings new challenges and revelations for Belly as she navigates friendships, romance, and family dynamics against the backdrop of the beach setting, which holds significant memories and emotional connections for her.
